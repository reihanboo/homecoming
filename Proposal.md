2. ## **Executive Summary**

Across Southeast Asia, millions of diaspora individuals live far from their countries of origin, carrying fragmented identities shaped by migration, displacement, and cultural distance. HOME-COMING is a heritage experience platform that connects diaspora individuals with curated, immersive tours in their home countries, pairing them with Cultural Ambassadors, local students and residents who share their background, to guide them through the experience.

Unlike conventional tourism, HOME-COMING is not about sightseeing. It is about belonging. Users do not visit their heritage country as tourists, they participate in it. Living in local communities, joining cultural workshops, contributing to local activities, and processing their identity through structured support including intergenerational trauma resources and Bicultural Affirmation programs.

The platform launches in Southeast Asia, with Palembang, Indonesia (ID) as the pilot city, with Vietnam (VN), the Philippines (PH), and Thailand (TH) as the first expansion markets. Revenue is generated through per-tour booking fees paid by users, and commission fees from local experience partners. The global diaspora market represents tens of millions of potential users across SEA alone, with growing demand for identity-driven travel experiences.

3. ## **Introduction**

According to the United Nations, approximately 281 million people live outside their country of origin globally \[1\], with Southeast Asia being one of the largest source regions. Vietnamese, Indonesian, Thai, and Filipino diaspora communities are among the most prominent, spread across the United States, Canada, Europe, Australia, and the Middle East.

For many of these individuals, particularly second and third-generation immigrants, the relationship with their home country is complicated. They may speak the language imperfectly, feel like outsiders when they visit, or carry the weight of family histories shaped by migration, conflict, or displacement. Existing tourism infrastructure does not address this. Regular tour packages treat all visitors the same, with no acknowledgment of the emotional and cultural complexity that diaspora individuals bring.

At the same time, local communities in SEA stand to benefit from deeper, more meaningful engagement with diaspora visitors, economically, culturally, and socially. There is a clear, underserved opportunity at the intersection of heritage tourism, cultural identity, and community development.

HOME-COMING is built to fill that gap.

4. ## **Business Description**

HOME-COMING is a web platform through which diaspora users discover, book, and experience curated heritage tours in their home countries. Each tour is designed around a specific cultural experience, an immersive community stay, a traditional craft or cuisine workshop, or a community participation activity, and comes with a paired Cultural Ambassador.

What makes it different from a travel agency is that a travel agency sells sightseeing. HOME-COMING sells belonging. The core product is not a tour itinerary, it is a structured reintegration experience, designed specifically for people who are returning to a place they are from but do not fully know. The Cultural Ambassador is not a tour guide reciting facts, they are a peer from the same heritage background who accompanies the user throughout the experience.

The product has three layers:

1. **The Experience**  
   The tour itself. Immersive stays (e.g. one month living in Palembang as a local resident), cultural workshops (batik, traditional cuisine, local music), or community participation activities (joining local development projects, festivals, or community programs).

2. **The Ambassador**  
   A local student or resident matched to the user based on shared heritage background, language, and the type of experience booked. The ambassador is a companion, not a guide.

3. **The Support Layer**  
   Resources for diaspora-specific challenges, for example intergenerational trauma (for families with histories of displacement or conflict), and Bicultural Affirmation, helping users accept and integrate both their heritage and adopted cultural identities rather than feeling torn between them.

5. ## **Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning**

The primary market is second and third-generation Southeast Asian diaspora living in the United States, Europe, Australia, and the Middle East, individuals who were born or raised outside their heritage country, speak the language partially or not at all, and feel a gap between their identity and their roots. This group is emotionally motivated to engage with a product like this and is currently underserved by existing tourism platforms. A secondary market is institutional. Universities, embassies, and cultural organizations that sponsor diaspora engagement programs.

The target customer is aged 20 to 45, a student or young professional, and is actively seeking cultural identity, reconnection with family heritage, or resolution of intergenerational family history. They are digitally literate and comfortable booking experiences online.

HOME-COMING is positioned as the only platform built exclusively for diaspora identity experiences, not general tourism. Where travel agencies offer packages for visitors, HOME-COMING offers experiences for people returning to somewhere they are from. Compared to platforms like Airbnb Experiences or Viator, HOME-COMING is narrower in audience and deeper in experience. It does not compete on volume; it competes on meaning.

6. ## **Demand and Competitor Analysis**

The Vietnamese diaspora numbers approximately 5.3 million people worldwide \[2\], the Indonesian diaspora approximately 4.5 million \[3\], the Filipino diaspora over 10 million \[4\], and the Thai diaspora approximately 1 million \[5\]. Heritage tourism is a growing segment of the global travel industry, valued at over USD 600 billion \[6\] and projected to grow as younger diaspora generations seek identity-driven experiences. Post-pandemic travel recovery has seen a strong uptick in purposeful travel \[7\], trips with emotional or cultural significance rather than pure leisure.

| Competitor | What They Offer | Gap |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| *Airbnb Experiences* | Local activities booked online | No diaspora focus, no ambassador pairing, no identity support |
| *Viator* / *GetYourGuide* | Tour booking platforms | Standard tourism, no heritage or emotional layer |
| Local travel agencies | Custom tour packages | Not scalable, no technology platform, no diaspora-specific design |

No existing platform directly addresses diaspora identity experiences at scale. *Airbnb Experiences* and *Viator* offer local activities and tours but have no diaspora focus, no ambassador pairing, and no identity or wellbeing support layer. Local travel agencies offer custom packages but are not scalable and have no technology platform. Government diaspora programs such as embassy-run return visits exist in some countries but are irregular, limited in scope, and not user-driven. HOME-COMING occupies a space none of these competitors currently fill.

7. ## **Operational Plan**

When a user creates a profile on the platform, they specify their heritage background, origin country, language preferences, and experience goals. They then browse available experiences filtered by country, type, and duration, and select and book one. A Cultural Ambassador from the same region and heritage background is assigned to the booking. The user travels and completes the experience with the Ambassador as their companion, with access to support resources throughout. After the experience, the user submits a review of both the experience and the Ambassador.

Cultural Ambassadors are recruited locally through university partnerships in pilot cities. They are trained in cultural facilitation and, where relevant, basic wellbeing support. Ambassadors operate as contractors and are compensated per completed booking. Each pilot city has a local coordinator responsible for ambassador recruitment, experience curation, and partner relations. Experience partners, local hosts, workshop facilitators, and community organizations, are onboarded and managed by the local coordinator.

8. ## **Marketing Strategy**

The core product is curated heritage experiences with Cultural Ambassador pairing, delivered through a web platform. Pricing varies by experience type and duration, with shorter workshops priced lower than extended immersive stays. Local experience partners pay a commission of 15 to 20 percent per booking made through the platform.

Promotion focuses on diaspora communities where they already gather, Facebook groups, TikTok, and Instagram communities in the United States, Australia, and Europe, using targeted campaigns built around identity and belonging rather than tourism. Partnerships with diaspora community organizations and cultural associations provide direct access to the target audience. Embassy and university partnerships support both institutional bookings and ambassador recruitment. The most powerful long-term channel is word-of-mouth from returned users sharing their experiences within their communities.

The platform is accessible globally for booking, with experiences physically located in SEA pilot cities. Local Cultural Ambassadors are the frontline of the product, and their quality directly determines user satisfaction. The end-to-end process, discovery, booking, ambassador matching, payment, support resources, and reviews, is managed entirely through the platform, building trust and social proof through verified reviews and user journey documentation.

9. ## **Business Analysis**

1. **SWOT Analysis**

   1. #### **Strengths**

**Highly Differentiated Value Proposition**: HOME-COMING shifts the paradigm from transactional mass tourism to emotional, identity-driven rehabilitation (Reminiscence Therapy) \[8\], creating an uncontested "Blue Ocean" market.

**Low-Cost, High-Trust Acquisition Network**: By leveraging international exchange student networks as primary organic channels, the platform achieves zero-budget marketing directly within high-trust diaspora community hubs.

**Asset-Light Scalability**: The "Phygital" platform model utilizes existing decentralized local infrastructure (grassroots homestays, rural craft cooperatives) allowing cross-border scaling without proportional operational liabilities..

2. #### **Weaknesses**

**Quality Assurance Dependency**: High reliance on the interpersonal skills and empathy of *Cultural Ambassadors*, requiring rigorous, standardized screening and continuous training protocols.

**High Initial Trust Barrier**: Reluctant adoption curves from older generations (like Generation 1.5) who harbor historical anxieties about returning to their homeland.

**Operational Friction in Unstructured Itineraries**: Personalized, open-ended stays inherently introduce logistical complexity regarding localized emergency response, safety, and regional insurance coordination.

3. #### **Opportunities**

**Untapped Regional Mega-Market:** Over 20 million Southeast Asian diaspora users represent an underserved market hungry for cultural repatriation, entirely ignored by mainstream commercial booking engines.

**Alignment with Global Tourism Shifts:** Strong macroeconomic tailwinds shifting consumer demand toward *Impact Travel*, *Regenerative Tourism*, and ESG-aligned social experiences.

**Strategic Diplomatic Synergy:** Massive potential to formalize Public-Private Partnerships (PPP) with Ministries of Foreign Affairs (e.g., Vietnam, Indonesia, Philippines, Thailand) to utilize their diplomatic reach and state-backed frameworks.

4. #### **Threats**

**Sovereign Geopolitical and Policy Risks:** Navigating complex, highly sensitive historical friction and varied cross-border immigration policies regarding displaced populations.

**Macroeconomic Volatility:** Fluctuation in global discretionary spending could limit participation from lower-income or student diaspora segments.

**Platform Disintermediation:** The risk of users and *Cultural Ambassadors* bypassing the platform to transact directly after the initial match is made.

**Political Backlash and Community Ostracism:** Radical or hardline political factions within certain overseas diaspora pockets may actively oppose the platform. Users who return via the platform risk facing harassment, boycotts, or being labeled as political targets within their local micro-communities abroad upon their return.

**Infiltration Risks:** The platform faces the threat of bad actors or political extremists exploiting the *Green Channel* to enter the host country with malicious, political destabilizing intentions.

2. **Business Model Canvas**

**Value Propositions:** 

HOME-COMING delivers a holistic ecosystem for identity healing and ancestral repatriation through structured, long-term digital counseling (*Reminiscence Therapy*) \[8\] paired with personalized, unstructured physical return journeys. It bridges historical divides by offering a state-sanctioned *Green Channel* that eliminates legal and psychological anxieties for the global diaspora, while simultaneously driving *Social Sustainability (SDG 11\)* \[9\] by injecting tourism revenue directly into remote, grassroots economies. 

**Regenerative Eco-Tourism & Capacity Building:** Transforming traditional farmers and regional artisans in off-the-grid destinations (e.g., Palembang) into active co-creators of eco-tourism. This directly generates sustainable livelihoods and decentralizes tourism revenue to vulnerable grassroots economies. 

**Customer Segments:**

* **Primary:** Multi-generational Southeast Asian Diaspora (Generations 1.5, 2, and 3\) experiencing identity alienation.  
* **Secondary:** Local grassroots communities, eco-tourism cooperatives, and rural artisans in underdeveloped regions.  
* **Institutional:** Ministries of Foreign Affairs, Embassies, and International Student Associations.

**Channels:**

HOME-COMING utilizes a multi-layered, trust-based **Omnichannel Acquisition Strategy** to bridge the gap between regional grassroots communities and the global diaspora:

* **Institutional & Grassroots Diaspora Networks (B2B2C Hubs):** The platform partners directly with established overseas community organizations (e.g., *Vietnamese/Indonesian/Thai/Filipino Student Associations, Diaspora Heritage Councils, and local cultural churches/temples*) in major host countries like Canada, the US, and Australia. These serve as high-trust, offline entry points to reach older generations (Gen 1.5) who prefer communal validation.

* **Institutional Student-Led Ambassador Program:** Leveraging the existing international exchange student networks as organic, peer-to-peer micro-influencers. These students act as the primary bridge, organizing localized "Heritage Coffee Workshops" and introducing the platform directly to diaspora elders in their immediate circles.


* **Hyper-Targeted Digital & Social Media Matrix:** 

  * **TikTok & Instagram (Visual Storytelling):** Utilizing short-form, high-emotion video content (curated from the *Heritage Gallery*) to target younger, second, and third-generation diaspora. Content focuses on cultural nostalgia, raw village aesthetics, and identity-searching journeys to trigger emotional resonance.

  * **Facebook (Community Building):** Engaging with existing, heavily populated diaspora groups and hometown association pages to foster organic word-of-mouth discussions and share long-form success stories of users who successfully returned.

* **Proprietary "Phygital" Platforms:** \* **The Specialized Web/App Interface:** Serving as the central hub where the *Heritage Gallery*, AI-driven emotional mapping, and the government-backed *ConsularTech* visa processing portal are integrated into a single, seamless user experience.

**Customer Relationships:** Built on strict privacy standards, high-empathy matching algorithms, continuous 24/7 localized support, and AI-driven content moderation to guarantee a safe, politically neutral space.

**Revenue Streams:**

* Premium community subscription tiers for extended digital counseling.  
* Merchandising of *Living Heritage Boxes* (curated, traceably sourced artisan crafts paired with digital storytelling).  
* Commission and service fees from customized *Root-Tracing* and *Remembrance* tours.

**Key Resources:** The proprietary matching platform, the trained *Cultural Ambassador* network, local eco-tourism partners, and official diplomatic backing/API integration for visa processing.

**Key Activities:** Continuous platform optimization, *ConsularTech* policy integration, ambassador recruitment/training, content moderation, and local community capacity building.

**Key Partnerships:** Ministries of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), global university exchange networks, remote rural cooperatives, and local historical/cultural preservation NGOs.

**Cost Structure:** Platform development and maintenance, ambassador compensation, localized insurance/safety operations, grassroot community equity sharing, and targeted diaspora marketing

3. **User Flow:**

**Step 1: Discovery (The Heritage Gallery)**  
The user browses authentic regional stories, local community media, and traditional artisan aesthetics uploaded by local youth. 

**Step 2: Emotional Mapping**  
The user registers and completes an *Emotional Readiness Assessment* to map their psychological comfort zones, cultural goals, and personal identity gaps. 

**Step 3: Long-term Digital Matching**  
The platform pairs the user 1-1 with a local Cultural Ambassador. They engage in asynchronous digital chat and voice notes for months to build mutual trust.

**Step 4: The Green Channel Activation**  
Once travel plans are formalized, the app triggers its ConsularTech portal, automatically aggregating data to fast-track official visa exemptions or diaspora cards through embassy APIs.

**Step 5: Unstructured Co-Creation (The Return)**  
The user arrives in the homeland and embarks on a flexible, low-profile, community-hosted itinerary with their Ambassador for emotional closure.
